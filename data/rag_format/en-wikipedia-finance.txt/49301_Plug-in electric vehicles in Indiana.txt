# Plug-in electric vehicles in Indiana



As of April 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 7,000 electric vehicles registered in Indiana.


## Government policy

As of February 2023ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, the state government does not offer any tax incentives for electric vehicle purchases. As of July 2023ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, the state government charges an annual registration fee of $214 for electric vehicles.


## Charging stations

As of 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 215 public AC level 2 charging stations and 19 DC charging stations in Indiana.

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, signed into law in November 2021, allocates nearly US$100 million to charging stations in Indiana.

In July 2021, the Indiana Department of Transportation and Purdue University began testing pavement that can charge electric vehicles while they are driving; this project is the first of its kind in the United States.


## Manufacturing

Indiana was historically a manufacturing hub for gasoline-powered cars, which has led many electric vehicle manufacturers to establish manufacturing hubs in the state.


## By region


### Indianapolis

As of October 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 400 public charging stations in the Indianapolis metropolitan area.


### Terre Haute

As of March 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were two public DC charging stations in Terre Haute.
