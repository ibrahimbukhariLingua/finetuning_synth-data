# Plug-in electric vehicles in Virginia

 

As of June 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 40,000 electric vehicles in Virginia, accounting for 0.5% of all vehicles in the state. As of August 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, 2% of new vehicles sold in the state were electric.


## Government policy

In 2021, the state government introduced a tax rebate of $2,500 for electric vehicle purchases. The same year, the state government introduced an annual $88.20 registration fee for electric vehicles.

The state government plans to require at least 8% of new cars sold in the state to be electric by 2024, will require all new cars sold to be electric by 2035.


## Charging stations

As of December 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 840 public charging station locations with 2,000 charging ports in Virginia. As of November 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 478 public DC charging ports in the state.

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, signed into law in November 2021, allocates US$105 million to charging stations in Virginia.


## By region


### Richmond

As of April 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, 2% of new cars registered in the Greater Richmond Region were electric.


### Washington metropolitan area

As of January 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 4,000 electric vehicles registered in Fairfax County. As of December 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 194 charging stations in Fairfax County.
