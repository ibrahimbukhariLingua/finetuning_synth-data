## Usage

In Indian English, the word is used both as an attributive and non-attributive noun with either an unmarked or marked ("-s") plural, respectively. For example: "1 *lakh* people"; "*lakhs* of people"; "20 *lakh* rupees"; "*lakhs* of rupees". In the abbreviated form, usage such as "â‚¹5L" or "â‚¹5 lac" (for "5 *lakh* rupees") is common. In this system of numeration, 100 *lakh* is called one *crore* and is equal to 10 million.

Formal written publications in English in India tend to use lakh/crore for Indian currency and Western numbering for foreign currencies, such as dollars and pounds.


### Silver market

The term is also used in the pricing of silver on the international precious metals market, where one *lakh* equals 100,000 troy ounces (3,100 kilograms) of silver.
