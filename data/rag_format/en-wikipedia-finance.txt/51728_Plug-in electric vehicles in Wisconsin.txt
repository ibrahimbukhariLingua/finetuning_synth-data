# Plug-in electric vehicles in Wisconsin



As of 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were about 9,000 electric vehicles registered in Wisconsin.


## Government policy

As of 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, the state government offers tax rebates of up to $1,000 for electric vehicle purchases.

As of 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, the state government charges a $100 registration fee for electric vehicles, and $75 for plug-in hybrid vehicles.


## Charging stations

As of February 2022ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 380 public charging stations in Wisconsin.

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, signed into law in November 2021, allocates US$79 million to charging stations in Wisconsin.

In June 2022, the state government released a plan to recognize I-90, I-94, I-43, I-41, I-535, US-53, and US-151 as alternative fuel corridors. Around half of all public charging stations in the state are within one mile of a corridor.


## By region


### Eau Claire

As of December 2019ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, less than 1% of vehicles registered in Eau Claire were electric.


### Madison

As of 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 2,227 electric vehicles registered in Dane County.


### Milwaukee

As of 2021ᵘᵖᵈᵃᵗᵉ, there were 1,320 electric vehicles registered in Milwaukee County.
